Purpose of the Research:
You are being invited to participate in a study of motor and cognitive processes conducted in the Intelligent Performance and Adaptation Laboratory, directed by Professor Jordan Taylor, in the Department of Psychology at Princeton University. Online Experiment: Aim For The Target!

You will be controlling a green dot with your trackpad. The green dot will sometimes be visible, and other times hidden. Even when you don't see the green dot, the game will always be tracking its position.

Please use a computer mouse or trackpad to complete the task.


NOTES:
    1. Once the game begins, you can quit the experiment at any time by pressing "Esc" and closing your browser tab or window.

    2. There are attention checks, so be sure to read all instructions thoroughly! Failing these checks will terminate the experiment.

    3. The experiment will be terminated if you do not respond to the given instructions or move the mouse to the target in a timely manner.

Instructions:

There are three simple steps to get through the experiment:

1. Use your mouse/trackpad to bring the green dot to the center of your screen.

A. The green dot will initially be hidden, and a yellow ring will indicate the distance between your cursor and the center of the screen (empty green circle). In the video below, a white mouse pointer is visible for illustrative purposes, but the pointer will not be visible during the experiment.


B. When the cursor is within a certain distance from the center, the green dot will become visible. When the cursor is at the center of the screen, the center circle will be filled.


2. Once the pink dot (your target) appears, slice through it FAST with the green dot (visible or hidden).


3. Repeat from step 1 until you have completed all 115 trials (trial counter shown in bottom left corner of the screen).


PLEASE READ: After several trials, the green dot will stop following the direction of your movements, and instead move on its own. When this happens, ignore the green dot and continue moving directly to the pink target. Throughout the entire experiment, you should be moving the mouse DIRECTLY to the pink target.
