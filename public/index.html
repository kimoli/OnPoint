Online Experiment: Aim For The Target!

You will be controlling a green dot with your trackpad. The green dot will sometimes be visible, and other times hidden. Even when you don't see the green dot, the game will always be tracking its position.

If possible, please use a trackpad to complete the task. If you don't have a trackpad, a computer mouse is fine.

The aiming game itself should take you 7-12 minutes to complete.


IMPORTANT INFORMATION:

1. To quit the experiment once the game begins, press "Esc" or exit fullscreen another way. Leaving fullscreen ends the HIT, and you will not be able to restart.

2. There are attention checks, so be sure to read all instructions thoroughly! Failing an attention check ends the HIT, and you will not be able to restart.

3. This experiment requires your full attention. If you do not respond in a timely manner, the HIT will end. You will not be able to restart.

Instructions:

There are three simple steps to get through the experiment:

1. Use your mouse/trackpad to bring the green dot to the white ring at the center of your screen. The colored wedges will guide you to the center.

A. The wedges show you what part of the screen your pointer is in. In the video below, a white mouse pointer is visible for illustrative purposes, but the pointer will not be visible during the experiment.


B. The wedges also show you how far from the white ring the pointer is.


C. When you get close enough to the white ring, you will see your cursor. Put your cursor inside the white ring to make it disappear.


2. Hold your cursor there until a pink dot (your target) is revealed. When you see the pink target, slice through it FAST with the green dot (visible or hidden).


3. Repeat from step 1 until you have completed all 115 trials (trial counter shown in bottom left corner of the screen).

PLEASE READ: After several trials, the green dot will stop following the direction of your movements, and instead move on its own. When this happens, ignore the green dot and continue moving directly to the pink target. Throughout the entire experiment, you should be moving the mouse DIRECTLY to the pink target.
